Psychology


I am a sophomore pyschology major from San Diego, California. I come from a family of athletes- my dad played college football at San Diego State University and my three brothers also play ball. Ever since I was little, I have been surrounded by sports. I initially grew up playing tackle football, but since I am a girl, I obviously had to find a different sport to be passionate about as I grew older. I ended up falling in love with softball at 12 years old. Ever since then, I have been playing competitively in Orange County, California, and have been training 4-6 days out of the week. Eventually, I became skilled enough to play at the collegiate level and earn myself a scholarship at the University of California, Riverside

A day in the life of a collegiate athlete is an absolute grind, to say the least. However, this experience has helped me grow as a person on and off the field, and I am loving who I am becoming. Throughout my experience, I have learned to be more independent, grateful, self-motivated, and hardworking. During the week, we are required to spend 20 hours playing softball- 4 hours a day during the weekdays. On top of this, we are required to go to class, and it is expected to maintain above a 3.0 GPA. Collegiate softball has taught me how to overcome obstacles while working with other people, to hold myself accountable to be a better person and player, and to be proactive.

Although I am a psychology major, I am interested in helping people become more satisfied with their physique. I am a good motivator and supporter, and would be a great strength and conditioning coach. Working with people and having a connection with them motivates me to help them be satisfied with their bodies. My experience makes me best fit to be a strength and conditioning coach.
